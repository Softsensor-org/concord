## Code vs. brand are different things

- **The code is open.** You may use, modify, fork, redistribute, and build
  commercial products on the Concord codebase, under the terms of Apache-2.0.
- **The name and logo are not licensed by Apache-2.0.** Section 6 of the Apache

  names, trademarks, or product names. The names **"Concord"** and

This is the same model used by many open-source projects (for example, the
Firefox source is open, but a rebuilt fork may not be distributed under the
"Firefox" name). Open code, protected brand.

## What you may do (nominative use)

- State, truthfully, that your product "works with," "is built on," or "is
  compatible with" Softsensor Concord.
- Refer to the project by name in articles, talks, documentation, and tutorials.
- Keep the existing project names and notices in unmodified copies you
  redistribute.

## What requires permission

- **Naming a fork or derivative "Concord" or "Softsensor Concord."** If you
  distribute a modified version, please give it a different name so users are not
  confused about its origin or who supports it.
- Using the Concord or Softsensor logos as the identity of your own product,
  service, or site.
- Using the marks in a way that implies endorsement, affiliation, or sponsorship
  by Softsensor when none exists.
- Registering domain names, social handles, or company names that incorporate
  the marks.

## Why this exists

The trademark protects users, not the code. It lets people trust that something
called "Softsensor Concord" actually comes from, or is supported by, the project
and Softsensor — while leaving everyone completely free to use and build on the
open code under whatever name they choose.
