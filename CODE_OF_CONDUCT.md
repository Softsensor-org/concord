# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We want participation in the Concord community to be a welcoming, harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of background or identity. As contributors
and maintainers, we pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an
open, friendly, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standard

This project adopts the **[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)**
as its code of conduct. The full text — including expected behavior, the
responsibilities of maintainers, scope, and the enforcement framework — is
maintained at that canonical link, and is incorporated here by reference.

In short: be respectful, assume good faith, accept constructive feedback
gracefully, and focus on what is best for the community. Behavior that
demeans, harasses, or excludes others is not welcome.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — the repository, issues,
pull requests, and any community channels — and also applies when an individual
is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it privately to the
maintainers at **opensource@softsensor.ai**. Reports will be reviewed and
investigated promptly and fairly, and the privacy and security of the reporter
will be respected.

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards and may
take any action they deem appropriate, up to and including a temporary or
permanent ban from the community, in response to behavior that violates this
Code of Conduct. The enforcement guidelines follow the Contributor Covenant 2.1
community-impact ladder linked above.
